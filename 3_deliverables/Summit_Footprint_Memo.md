**Subject:** Synthesized footprint, ownership, and growth strategy for Summit School Services, LLC (formerly National Express School / NEXS; primary US operating brand: Durham School Services)

---

## 1) Current Ownership (well-sourced)

- **Owner:** **I Squared Capital** — global infrastructure investor (~$40–45B AUM, ~90 portfolio companies across 70+ countries). Summit is I Squared's first US school transportation platform.
- **Acquired from:** **Mobico Group plc** (UK-listed; formerly National Express Group plc, renamed 2023).
- **Deal economics:** Up to **~$608M enterprise value** (cash + earn-out / contingent consideration); £457M equivalent.
- **Timing:** Announced **March/April 2025** (post a longer pre-marketing period dating to late 2023); US Surface Transportation Board clearance **July 8, 2025**; closed **July 14–15, 2025**, ahead of Sept. 30 long-stop date.
- **Rebrand:** Corporate parent rebranded to **Summit School Services, LLC** in Q3 2025; legacy operating brands (Durham, Petermann, Stock, A&S, Septran, etc.) retained at the depot/contract level. `nellc.com` now 301-redirects to `summitllc.com`.
- **HQ:** 27755 Diehl Rd., Suite 100, **Warrenville, IL 60555**.
- **CEO:** **Tim Wertner** (in role since June 2023; retained post-transaction).

## 6) Growth / Tuck-in Acquisition Strategy

### Historical (NEXG/Mobico era, 1999–2025) — well-sourced
Platform was built via **roll-up M&A**:
- **1999** Durham Transportation (~$175M)
- **2000** School Services & Leasing
- **2002** Stock Transportation (Canada)
- **2012/2018** Petermann Partners (Cincinnati; lifted NEXG US fleet to ~20,000)
- Smaller local operators folded into the "12 local transportation companies" structure over time

### 2023–2025 (Wertner pre-sale turnaround) — well-sourced
- Shed loss-making contracts → fleet shrank from ~22,500 to ~14,000 buses
- Raised contract pricing above inflation, reinstated profitable routes
- Driver recruitment/retention recovered to **104% staffing** entering SY 2025–26
- **Margin-over-volume** strategy

### Post-2025 (I Squared / Summit) — mix of stated + inference
**Stated priorities (sourced from CEO interviews and press):**
1. Recover lost routes
2. Secure new district contracts (organic wins in FL, NY, PA, MO, GA, IN already announced)
3. Driver recruitment/retention
4. Above-inflation contract pricing
5. Geographic footprint expansion
6. **Fleet electrification** — 600K+ cumulative EV miles; ~91 EVs in Cumberland RI, Compton CA, New Orleans LA; +140 EVs slated for FL, MI, MA by mid-2026 (Highland Electric Fleets partnership); EPA Clean School Bus Program funding
7. **Technology** — proprietary **"Catalyst"** Innovation Hub (predictive analytics, fleet visibility) launching Q1 2026

**Inferred tuck-in M&A posture:**
- I Squared publicly describes its model as "acquiring and consolidating mid-market companies" and "developing investment platforms…that start small and grow big" — Summit fits the **platform-build playbook**.
- The 12-brand "house of local brands" structure is **purpose-built for absorbing regional/family-owned bus contractors** while preserving local goodwill.
- CEO Wertner: *"The acquisition itself is the strength we can bring to our partners."*
- **No specific tuck-in pipeline or post-close bolt-on has been publicly announced** as of June 2026; near-term messaging emphasizes organic route wins, electrification capex, and tech rollout.
- **Likely consolidator economics:** distressed/exiting regional operators (post-COVID margin pressure across the sector) folded into Summit's shared safety/tech/procurement stack — analogous to Student Transportation Inc. and First Student under PE/infra ownership.
